# Session 08 — Sister Garaele and the Necromancer
#sessions #player-safe

**Date played:** Unknown — early campaign
**Location:** Phandalin → Old Owl Well → Defiance Draw (ridge only)

---

The party returned to Phandalin before heading overland toward Old Owl Well, where an anonymous complaint had pointed them toward a necromancer disturbing the dead near the ruins.

The necromancer — a human wizard named Haman Kostyk — was camped at Old Owl Well with a research setup and considerably more company than the living kind. He had been using the site for what he called research. The party interrupted him mid-explanation. Before any negotiation could conclude, a shadow figure stepped from nowhere, killed Kostyk cleanly, took his staff, and vanished. The same figure had appeared once before, at a campfire ambush, and done exactly the same thing to exactly the same result. Nobody knows who they are.

Kostyk's camp held a journal, a history of Defiance Draw, a handful of useful items, and a great deal of unresolved questions about what exactly he had been doing and why.

His journal — recovered from the camp — told a more complicated story than his campsite suggested. He had started as a grieving younger brother, lost his sibling to an undiagnosed illness, and spent thirty years trying to find a cure through increasingly unorthodox methods. By the end he had rationalized his way into something darker, but the grief underneath it was real. He was close to something when he died — something in Defiance Draw that he believed was significant enough that he wouldn't write it down for fear of jinxing it.

From a ridge above the valley, the party looked out over Defiance Draw for the first time: a flooded basin, scattered islands, a single structure standing at the center, the Swordstorm Mountains beyond. They noted it. They moved on. The flute pulled them into the Feywild shortly after, and they never went back.

Defiance Draw is still there.
