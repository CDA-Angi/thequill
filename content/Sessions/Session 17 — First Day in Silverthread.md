# Session 17 — First Day in Silverthread
#sessions #player-safe

**Date played:** March 21, 2026
**Location:** Silverthread — The Aureate, The Tangle, The Umbral (attempted)

---

Session 17 was the party's first full day in the city. They explored the Aureate in the morning light — the Silverflow Fountain, Tallowmere Provisions, glimpses of the Gilt Market — before the day opened into something stranger.

At the Silverspan, they stopped to watch an argument. Brenn Asholt, the city's own cartographer, sat at a drafting table with Lady Caelindra Voss standing over him. She wanted a bridge added to his official map. He refused. Not because he doubted it existed — he used it every day for a hundred and fifty years — but because the official record contained no bridge, and his survey had to reflect the official record. Those were two different problems. Only one of them was his to solve.

The party loved him immediately.

They attempted the Umbral and were turned back — too early, district not yet active. The curiosity that produced will come back around. Lucian and Dash were already eyeing Willow's map of the city, already thinking about the Unseelie Archive.

At the Court of Petals gardens, something else happened. The architecture at the back of the platform didn't fit — mismatched stonework, a gap in the design that felt more like an absence than a mistake. While the others examined it, Clever made a series of jumps across the gap to the Umbral platform and back, finding a route through open air that shouldn't have been there. She didn't know what she'd done. She just found a path that used to be a path.

The Twilight Rest visit produced a crystal ashtray in Cyrene's pocket and the knowledge that the proprietor, Esovin, always knows where everyone in the room is. That thread is sitting quietly for now.

Then Liraelle.

She performed somewhere the party could hear her — a quiet space, nothing formal. Her music was extraordinary and something else underneath, desperate in a way that didn't fit the melody. After, she showed them what she'd been carrying. Two programs from a coronation two hundred years ago. Both authentic. Both from her own hand. Both bearing different names.

While she was holding one of them, it changed. Not gradually. The name on it shifted from one to the other while she was holding it, seal intact, no one touching it.

The party watched it happen.

They checked into the Silver Bough Inn — two rooms, ten gold total — and Aldric Moss showed them upstairs. Somewhere above the city, the sky kept shifting, settling nowhere, neither gold nor violet winning the argument.

---

*What you know now that you didn't before:*

A bridge once connected the Aureate and Umbral directly, bypassing the Tangle. Most people have no memory of it. Brenn does. Caelindra has a map. The place where it connected still shows something — in the stonework, in the space itself.

And documents can change. Remotely. Without being touched. You watched it happen.
