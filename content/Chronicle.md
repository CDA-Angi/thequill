# Chronicle of Certain Witnesses: Canonical Annotations
#chronicle #player-safe #public

---

*What follows is drawn from an ongoing record of subjects flagged for canonical significance. Entries prior to the subjects' crossing into the Feywild are fragmentary. The record does not concern itself with ordinary lives. It concerns itself with witnesses. The record thickens accordingly when arriving at a specific, documented moment.*

*Gaps in the early entries are noted where they occur. They are not errors.*

---

**[[Session 01 — The Ambush on the Triboar Trail]]**
A group of travelers came together on the road to Phandalin. A job, a wagon, a dwarf who had found something and needed people to protect it. The road was dangerous in the ordinary ways. They handled it.

---

**[[Session 02 — Exploring Cragmaw Cave]]**
They followed goblins to a cave and dealt with what they found there. They were moving through the world, as many do, and the world was moving around them.

---

**[[Session 03 — Arrival in Phandalin]]**
A small settlement, troubled. They arrived and began to understand its problems.

---

**[[Session 04 — Investigating the Redbrand Hideout]]**
A gang operating out of an old manor. They investigated. They dealt with it.

---

**[[Session 05 — Return to Phandalin]]**
They uncovered more of what was happening beneath the surface — threads connecting to a figure called the Black Spider and a dwarf who had disappeared.

---

**[[Session 06 — Conyberry and the Road South]]**
The road took them to Conyberry, a village long since abandoned. They picked through its ruins. Clever found claw marks in the floorboards of an old trading post — four long gouges, too large for anything ordinary. A curved fang lay nearby.

On the road south, cockatrices ambushed them. Two strangers appeared to help: Rithlin, a wood elf druid, and Shydwyn, a wood elf ranger. Matuk was petrified. That night, around a fire, Rithlin told stories. Shydwyn told a story about wights that had burned her village when she was a child. The camp was attacked before morning — a mage, a summoned demon, and a shadow that killed the mage, took his staff, and vanished.

In the morning, Rithlin gave the party a sealed wooden box and asked them to deliver it to a woman named Agatha.

---

**[[Session 07 — Agatha the Banshee]]**
Agatha had been a banshee for over a thousand years. She received them with the particular haughtiness of someone who had been dead long enough to stop finding it interesting.

She was a diviner, trapped. She traded answers for life force. She remembered the villagers of Conyberry fondly. She told them about a book that had turned her into a banshee, hidden somewhere in Cragmaw Castle. She wanted it back. She wanted, after a thousand years, to be free.

When they gave her Rithlin's sealed box, something shifted in her expression. She recognized the seal. "He's still around, huh," she said, and didn't explain further.

**[[Session 08 — Sister Garaele and the Necromancer]]**
Before leaving for Old Owl Well, Dash cast Detect Magic on Sister Garaele and found a faint glow around her finger and something hidden in her pouch. The observation was noted and not acted on.

The necromancer at Old Owl Well was named Haman Kostyk. He was in the middle of negotiating with the party when a shadow appeared and killed him cleanly, taking his staff. The party investigated his camp. Dash found a book: a history of Defiance Draw. The book described a border station between two empires, a war, a miscast spell, and a catastrophic flood. Wave Echo Cave had become inaccessible after the spell. From a ridge, they looked out over Defiance Draw for the first time.

---

**[[Session 09 — Entering Defiance Draw]]**
They entered the Draw. Strange terrain, floating stone formations, a flooded valley at its heart. In the northeast corner, in a still meadow surrounded by flowers, they found a flat rock with a flute resting on top of it.

Dash began casting Detect Magic. Before the spell completed, Silenus picked up the flute and played it.

The meadow changed. Winged creatures filled the air. The light shifted gold. A woman with pointed ears and a crown of twigs rode in on an elk, addressed Silenus as her artist, and left before they could properly respond.

They camped in the changed meadow. Silenus played the flute into the night.

---

**[[Session 10 — Arrival in the Feywild]]**
Morning in the Feywild. Fap was standing beside Silenus when he woke, watching him silently.

They found a river and followed it to a reverse waterfall — two hundred feet of water falling upward. They rappelled down one by one. Silenus fell. They entered the forest beyond, which bowed the trees out of their path as they walked.

In a clearing, a single tree stood at the center. Small twig-like creatures emerged from the undergrowth and surrounded them. They didn't attack. Each one issued a personal challenge.

---

**[[Session 11 — The Twig Jesters]]**
Each of them rose to their challenge in their own way.

Matuk answered with thunderous roars and earned a Whisperseed. Clever used her tail in a pantomime of theft and was granted the ability to disguise herself as Fey. Luna untangled a riddle-poem and received a pearly stone that glows in the presence of lies. Dash spread spectral wings from a great height and gained a map that shifts when rhymes are spoken. Silenus delivered a monologue about loss and was given an invitation to the Unseelie Court.

What they received bound them, in ways they did not yet understand, to a fixed version of what was real.

*Notation: Challenges issued and met. Anchors granted. The record is now active.*

**[[Session 12 — The Singing Pools]]**
An endless party at the edge of a still body of water. The drinks refilled before glasses emptied. The partygoers seemed unable — or unwilling — to leave.

Luna went under the water as an octopus and found the truth: the corpse of a Jabberwock at the mouth of a cavern below, being drained into bottles and carried up by nymphs. The party was the only group there who understood what they were drinking. They chose not to stop it. Not yet.

The Fey road home led somewhere else: a vast golden chasm, floating stone islands, radiant jellyfish drifting in glowing mist below. They jumped. Most of them fell at least once. Matuk fell into the mist and floated there for a moment beside one of the creatures before being pulled back.

On the far side, a structure woven into the roots of a massive tree. A sign in Sylvan above the door read *The Court of Echoes.* A woman in black robes stitched with starlight greeted them.

*"Welcome, travelers. I am Velistra, the Threshold Archivist. May I take your names... or perhaps your echoes?"*

**[[Session 14 — Auntie's Boys and the Unicorn Abduction]]**
They followed a Feywild trail to a ruined alchemist's hut. A tiefling rogue named Cyrene was watching it from the treeline, looking for what she called "Auntie's boys." Inside, they found a locked chest and a hidden alchemical cache. Working together, they followed the missing alchemist's recipe and succeeded in making healing potions on their first try.

Beyond the hut, the swamp grew thick. They found a dead man's bones, arranged with care, and beside them an orb. The arrangement was too deliberate to be accidental.

Two flesh golems arrived. Cyrene killed the first. Matuk killed the second. Dash sketched their remains.

Then a wounded unicorn stumbled toward them from the mist, a violet rift tearing open behind it. A masked, stag-like creature dragged the unicorn through the rift. The rift closed.

Applause followed. Two elegant elves were seated in a nearby tree, watching. One spoke: *"You're going to want to go that way."* They vanished.

**[[Session 16 — Road to Silverthread]]**
A quiet morning at an inn. A pale blue Eladrin scholar named Ilex joined their table and invited them to walk with him toward a city whose sky had grown unstable. They agreed.

The road was strange in the ways Feywild roads are strange: reversed birdsong, shifting flowers, a rabbit that crossed the path four times and dissolved. At a stone bridge over a still, currentless stream, the hag arrived with Redcaps — not the flesh golems, but creatures made the same way, every stitch and seam. The party killed them. The hag came out of the trees, looked at each of them in turn, attacked, was wounded, and stepped back into shadow. The sound she left behind might have been weeping.

Ilex noted she had not come to kill them. She had come to see them. The distinction meant she still thought of them as something.

Silverthread rose before them as the trees thinned: silver birch towers, a sky cycling between amber and violet. At the Messagery, Matuk exchanged a tooth and a favor for a door home. A seam of gold-white light opened. He stepped through. It closed behind him.

Ilex parted from them at the crossroads with a last quiet piece of advice: *Pay attention to the city. Not just the people. The city.*

---

**[[Session 17 — Exploring Silverthread]]**
The party's first full day in the city. They moved through the Aureate, left an offering at the Silverflow Fountain, tried the Woven Cup, were turned away from the Umbral.

At the Silverspan, a cartographer named Brenn Asholt was arguing with a Seelie noble about a bridge that no longer existed. He had used it every day for a hundred and fifty years. Most people he spoke to had no memory of it at all. Those were two different problems. Only one of them was his to solve.

In the Court of Petals gardens, Clever found a route through empty air between the two quarters and jumped it. She did not know what she had crossed.

Then Liraelle Brightstring showed them two programs from a coronation two hundred years ago. Both authentic. Both bearing different names. While she was holding one of them, the name changed. Seal intact. No one touching it.

The party watched it happen.

*Notation: First direct witness event for this subject group. The record confirms what was already known.*

**[[Session 18 — The Merchant Wagon]]**
A job from Commander Arran: a merchant wagon two days overdue on the Goldwalk road.

They found the wagon and three bodies. The attack was consistent with trained displacer beasts, deliberately directed. The cargo manifest didn't match the weight. A second ledger notation in a different hand: *S. — Inkwell — urgent.* The Echo Stone showed Dash what happened: an elf in forest leathers, a deliberate interception, the elf killed by his own animals when something went wrong.

Someone sent those beasts to stop that shipment. The person they sent is dead. What was in the shipment is gone.

On the way back, a clearing off the road. Four displacer beasts in a loose circle around a wounded juvenile owlbear. And inside the circle, a body.
